Marie Curie's Life and Achievements

Marie Curie, original name is Maria Sklodowska, was a Polish and naturalized-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering for the science world.

Timeline of Her life

  1. Born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland.
  2. Moved to Paris, France, in 1895 for better education
  3. Discoverd polonium and radium in 1898
  4. Won nobel prizes in physics(1903) and chemistry (1911)
  5. Died on July 4, 1934

Marie Curie had a great interest in mathematics and physics. However, due to her gender and economic reasons, she could not receive higher education in her native Poland, so she moved to Paris to complete her studies.

While studying in Paris, she met Pierre Curie, a professor at the School of Physics, and they got married the following year.
From then on, she began her difficult journey as a scientist.

Challenges in Life: Despite her extraordinary achievements, Marie Curie faced significant societal prejudice as a female scientist, challenging gender norms while excelling in a male-dominated field. She experienced tremendous physical and mental stress in her pursuit of scientific research. Prolonged exposure to radioactive substances also seriously affected her health.
